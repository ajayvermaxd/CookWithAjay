Certainly! Here's a vegetarian Chop Suey recipe:

**Ingredients:**

For the sauce:
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (substitute with vegetarian oyster sauce if desired)
- 1 teaspoon sugar
- 1/2 cup vegetable broth

For the stir-fry:
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 1 small onion, thinly sliced
- 1 bell pepper, thinly sliced
- 1 carrot, thinly sliced
- 1 cup sliced mushrooms
- 1 cup sliced bok choy or Chinese cabbage
- 1 cup snow peas, trimmed
- 1 cup bean sprouts
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Cooked rice or noodles for serving

**Instructions:**

**For the Sauce:**

1. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, oyster sauce (or vegetarian oyster sauce), sugar, and vegetable broth. Set the sauce aside.

**For the Stir-Fry:**

1. Heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.

2. Add the minced garlic and ginger to the hot oil and stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant.

3. Add the thinly sliced onions and stir-fry for 1-2 minutes until they become translucent.

4. Add the sliced bell pepper, carrot, and mushrooms to the skillet. Stir-fry for a few minutes until the vegetables start to soften but remain crisp.

5. Toss in the sliced bok choy or Chinese cabbage, snow peas, and bean sprouts. Continue stir-frying for another 2-3 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp.

6. Pour the prepared sauce over the stir-fried vegetables.

7. Stir-fry everything together for an additional 2-3 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly, and the vegetables are evenly coated.

8. Taste the stir-fry and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper if needed.

**To Serve:**

- Serve your Vegetarian Chop Suey hot over cooked rice or noodles.

Enjoy your homemade Vegetarian Chop Suey! Feel free to customize it with your favorite vegetables or tofu for added protein.
